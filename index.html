PhD Student — Cognitive & Psychological Sciences, Brown University

I am a PhD student working with Oriel FeldmanHall at Brown University. My work sits at the intersection of individual differences, emotional dynamics, and mental health.

Previously, I was a full-time research assistant with Dean Mobbs at Caltech and Sarah Tashjian at the University of Melbourne. I completed my undergraduate training at Emory University, where I double-majored in Neuroscience and Anthropology under the mentorship of James Riling.

Research Interests

Loneliness & Social Networks

How neural representations of social networks predict chronic loneliness, and how network instability over time relates to loneliness trajectories using longitudinal ESM data and network modeling.

Emotional Dynamics

Individual differences in emotional dynamics as potential risk factors for mood disorders, and how chronic loneliness alters the temporal structure of emotion.

Social Cognition & fMRI

Neural mechanisms of social feature learning, emotion prediction errors, and how the brain encodes familiarity and friendship structure in real-world social networks.

AI & Mental Health

Understanding how large language models represent latent psychological states — with the goal of informing the design of safe and effective AI companions for mental health.
